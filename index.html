About Me

Hello! I'm a Space and Astronomy CSIRO Early Research Career Postdoctoral Fellow in the Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence Future Science Platform (MLAI FSP). My current research involves using ML and AI algorithms for finding the rare and unknowns in astronomical datasets.

I completed my PhD in Astrophysics and Masters of Science (Physics) from the University of Melbourne under the supervision of Professor Rachel Webster. My thesis focused on exploring the observable properties of quasars using simulations, statistical methods, and ML techniques.

My research fields are in astronomy, astrophysics, ML nd AI. My interests include quasars broad line region and application of statistics, ML, AI, and data science techniques to solve real-world problems.

Research

List of current research projects

Finding the Unknowns in Astronomical Datasets with Machine Learning Techniques

In collaboration with CSIRO Space and Astronomy, Data61 FSP, UWS

  • The hunt for any unexpected or significant radio signals, including those that could be astronomical or from (plausible) extraterrestrial intelligence, in the Murriyang (Parkes) telescope data.
  • Develop machine learning algorithms to detect these signals.
  • Further application to major radio surveys, such as the Australian Square Kilometre Array Pathfinder (ASKAP) and Evolutionary Map of the Universe (EMU).
  • The discovery would be of major impact to human kind!

Quasar Disk-wind Phenomenology

In collaboration with Rachel Webster (UniMelb)

  • Combine observations and modelling in a comprehensive review of the ultraviolet-optical emission region of quasar spectra.
  • Understand the geometry and physics of the quasar emission region.

Galaxy 3D Shape Recovery using Neural Networks

In collaboration with Caroline Foster (USyd), Kate Harborne (ICRAR/UWA), Robert Bassett (CSL)

  • Further extension from galaxy shape recovery project to include a range of mock simulation data, while ensuring equal representation of each galaxy projection.
  • These will be used to train the neural network model to predict the shape of the galaxy.
